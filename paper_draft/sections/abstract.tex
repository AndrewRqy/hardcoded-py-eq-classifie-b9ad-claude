We study the problem of classifying equational implication over magmas: given
two equational laws $E$ and $E'$ over a single binary operation, determine whether
every magma satisfying $E$ also satisfies $E'$.  This question arises at scale in
the Equational Theories Project, which has verified all $22{,}028{,}942$ pairwise
implications among $4{,}694$ laws of order at most four.

We present \textsc{predictor4}, a layered decision procedure that replaces two
unsound or incomplete components of its predecessor with theoretically grounded
alternatives.  For the positive implication path, Knuth-Bendix (KB) completion
replaces a bounded breadth-first reachability search, providing a proof procedure
that is \emph{sound by construction} when it terminates.  For the negative path,
an SMT-based finite model finder using Z3 replaces a heuristic hill-climbing search,
providing \emph{completeness} for each fixed domain size.

A central contribution is the identification and formal analysis of a \emph{soundness
invariant} for KB completion: every rewrite rule $\ell \to r$ in the generated term
rewriting system must satisfy $\operatorname{vars}(r) \subseteq \operatorname{vars}(\ell)$.
We prove that violation of this invariant causes all terms to collapse to the same
normal form, making the normal-form equality test trivially true and producing false
positives.  We state this as a precise theorem and provide its proof.

Empirical evaluation on stratified samples of 50 and 200 cases from the ground-truth
implication matrix confirms that \textsc{predictor4}, after enforcing the invariant,
achieves $100\%$ accuracy with zero false positives and zero false negatives, matching
its predecessor on both sample sizes while providing strictly stronger theoretical
guarantees for the positive implication path.
